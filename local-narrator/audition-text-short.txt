The first rule of Wintersong waters was simple: do not fall in. The second was less comforting. If you did fall in, try to die quickly, because the sea between Icehaven and Exile had opinions about people who took too long.

Nix had spent six days on Orra's trading boat pretending to be cargo that complained. Officially, she was helping carry cold medicines, thermal cloth, and salt blocks through the outer routes. Unofficially, The Ferryman had sent her to count prison ships—not interfere with them, board them, or develop strong feelings about them. Count them. He wanted times, routes, flags, guard numbers, relay habits, and the names of captains who drank enough to become careless before the Icehaven line.
