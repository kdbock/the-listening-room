People liked to pretend that warnings existed to keep them safe. Usually, they existed so someone could say they had warned you afterward. Nix had learned the distinction young, along with several less useful lessons involving locked doors, borrowed knives, and men who mistook silence for agreement.

She trusted a good map, a sharp blade, and plans with enough room for betrayal. Everything else required inspection.

That did not make her cynical. It made her difficult to surprise, which was better.
