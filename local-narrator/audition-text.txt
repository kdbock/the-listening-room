The first rule of Wintersong waters was simple: do not fall in. The second was less comforting. If you did fall in, try to die quickly, because the sea between Icehaven and Exile had opinions about people who took too long.

Nix had spent six days on Orra's trading boat pretending to be cargo that complained. Officially, she was helping carry cold medicines, thermal cloth, and salt blocks through the outer routes. Unofficially, The Ferryman had sent her to count prison ships—not interfere with them, board them, or develop strong feelings about them. Count them. He wanted times, routes, flags, guard numbers, relay habits, and the names of captains who drank enough to become careless before the Icehaven line.

It was the sort of work Nix usually liked: quiet, useful, and mostly performed from a distance with a notebook, a spyglass, and enough food to suggest she had planned ahead. Later, she would understand that The Ferryman had not sent her north to count ships. He had sent her to notice the one he already meant to steal.

Orra hated having her aboard. This was not personal. Orra hated wasted heat, bad knots, weak hulls, sentimental mainlanders, anyone who touched her wheel, and Nix after the Bellwater lantern incident. In fairness, the lantern had been too close to the powder casks before Nix became involved. It had also been considerably less intact afterward.

"You said it was a controlled distraction," Orra had told her while they bailed river water out of the hold with cooking pots.

"It was controlled briefly."
