## Creative direction

The cold should feel intelligent rather than merely unpleasant. The world is dangerous, but Nix’s dry humor remains the center of the performance. Sound supports the turns in the prose and does not illustrate every noun, footstep, knife, or joke.

This pilot uses:

- one continuous but evolving northern-water ambience;
- two brief musical statements and one final tension movement;
- four prominent designed effects;
- silence and reduced ambience around punch lines and the Ferryman’s orders.

## Sound-generation and search language

These descriptions can be used either as library search terms or as prompts for generated effects.

### Northern-water ambience

> Arctic open-water ambience from a small wooden trading boat, cold steady wind, dark water lapping softly against timber, occasional distant ice pressure and sparse ice contact, bleak and spacious, no birds, no harbor, no voices, no large waves, seamless loop

### Token signature

> A short intimate magical pulse from a warm brass token hidden beneath a heavy coat, muted metallic resonance with a tiny crystalline core, ancient precision rather than modern electronics, dry close sound, no beep, no sparkle shower, one-second decay

### Message activation

> An uncanny internal communication opening behind the listener’s eye, the brass-token pulse folding inward into a low controlled resonance, subtle neurological pressure, elegant and ancient, no computer interface, no glitch, no cinematic boom

### Opening music

> Dark polar fantasy underscore, one low bowed string tone and faint glass harmonic, glacial spaciousness, intelligent danger, restrained literary drama, no percussion, no choir, no heroic melody, loopable or clean ending

### The order

> Minimal dark-fantasy tension underscore, slow low pressure pulse, controlled authority and approaching danger, sparse texture, no trailer drums, no ostinato, no vocals, designed to sit quietly beneath spoken narration

## Mix boundaries for the pilot

- Preserve the current narration WAV as the untouched master.
- Put ambience, spot effects, and music on separate tracks.
- Begin with music roughly 22–26 dB below the narration and ambience roughly 26–30 dB below it; adjust by ear after measuring the narration.
- Duck music and ambience under speech instead of raising the narration to fight them.
- Spot effects may briefly become clearer, but they must never mask the leading consonant of the next sentence.
- Judge the mix first on ordinary phone speakers, then headphones. If an effect is impressive on headphones but distracting on a phone, reduce it.
- Export both a clean-narration edition and a sound-designed edition.

## Approval question for the pilot

The meaningful creative decision is density: this sheet proposes **six essential sound moments across 3:43**, plus ambience and restrained music. The first mixed pilot uses original locally synthesized audio for every added layer. If its density feels right, it becomes the working sound language for the remaining 35 parts.
